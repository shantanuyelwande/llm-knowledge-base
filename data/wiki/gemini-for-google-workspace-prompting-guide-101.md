# Gemini for Google Workspace Prompting Guide

## Summary

A comprehensive guide to writing effective prompts for [[Gemini]] AI assistant within [[Google Workspace]] applications. The guide provides foundational prompting skills organized around four key components: persona, task, context, and format. It includes role-specific examples for 12+ professional personas and emphasizes iterative refinement, natural language, and human review of AI outputs.

---

## Overview

**Gemini for Google Workspace Prompting Guide** is an official Google handbook (October 2024 edition) designed to help users collaborate effectively with [[artificial intelligence]] across integrated [[Google Workspace]] applications including [[Gmail]], [[Google Docs]], [[Google Drive]], [[Google Sheets]], [[Google Meet]], [[Google Slides]], and [[Gemini Advanced]].

The guide establishes that [[prompt engineering]] is a learnable skill accessible to all users, not just technical specialists. It emphasizes that effective prompts average around 21 words with relevant context, though most users typically write prompts under nine words.

## Core Prompting Framework

### Four Essential Components

The guide identifies four main areas to consider when writing effective prompts:

1. **Persona** - The role or perspective you want the AI to assume
2. **Task** - The specific action or command you want performed (most critical component)
3. **Context** - Background information and relevant details
4. **Format** - The desired structure or presentation of the output

Not all four components are required for every prompt, but using multiple components yields better results.

### Example Framework Prompt

```
You are a [persona]. [Task] based on [context]. [Format requirement].
```

A practical example: "You are a program manager in [industry]. Draft an executive summary email to [persona] based on [details about relevant program docs]. Limit to bullet points."

## Best Practices for Effective Prompting

### Six Core Tips

1. **Use Natural Language** - Write as if speaking to another person; express complete thoughts in full sentences

2. **Be Specific and Iterate** - Tell Gemini what to do (summarize, write, change tone, create); provide as much context as possible

3. **Be Concise and Avoid Complexity** - State requests in brief but specific language; avoid jargon

4. **Make It a Conversation** - Fine-tune prompts through follow-up prompts; use iterative review and refinement

5. **Use Your Documents** - Personalize outputs by referencing personal files in [[Google Drive]] using @file notation

6. **Use Prompt Editor Feature** - In [[Gemini Advanced]], start with "Make this a power prompt: [original prompt]" to receive improvement suggestions

### Advanced Prompting Techniques

- **Break It Up** - Separate related tasks into multiple prompts for better results
- **Give Constraints** - Include specific limits like character count or number of options
- **Assign a Role** - Start prompts with role descriptions to encourage creativity
- **Ask for Feedback** - In conversations, ask Gemini clarifying questions about what would improve output
- **Consider Tone** - Specify desired tone (formal, informal, technical, creative, casual)
- **Say It Another Way** - Rephrase prompts if initial results don't meet expectations

## Critical Guidance on AI Output Review

### Before Implementation

The guide emphasizes that users must:

1. **Review All Output** - Ensure clarity, relevance, and accuracy before use
2. **Verify Information** - Check facts and claims independently
3. **Maintain Human Responsibility** - Remember that "generative AI is meant to help humans, but the final output is yours"
4. **Acknowledge Unpredictability** - Recognize that AI models can produce unpredictable responses
5. **Accept Responsibility** - Users bear final responsibility for any content they publish or act upon

### Iterative Refinement Process

The guide recommends:
- Multiple prompt attempts if initial results don't meet expectations
- Follow-up prompts to refine and improve outputs
- Conversation-based refinement rather than single-prompt reliance
- Testing different prompt phrasings and approaches

## Prompt Iteration Example

The guide illustrates iterative prompting through an administrative support scenario:

**Initial Prompt**: "I am an executive administrator to a team director. Our newly formed team now consists of content marketers, digital marketers, and product marketers. We are gathering for the first time at a three-day offsite in Washington, DC. Plan activities for each day that include team bonding activities and time for deeper strategic work. Create a sample agenda for me."

**Follow-up Prompt 1**: "Suggest three different icebreaker activities that encourage people to learn about their teammates' preferred working styles, strengths, and goals. Make sure the icebreaker ideas are engaging and can be completed by a group of 25 people in 30 minutes or less."

**Follow-up Prompt 2**: "Organize this agenda in a table format. Include one of your suggested icebreakers for each day."

**Follow-up Prompt 3**: "Use @[2024 H2 Team Vision] to generate a summary for the opening remarks on Day 1 of this agenda."
